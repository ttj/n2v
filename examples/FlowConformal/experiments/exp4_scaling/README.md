# Exp 4: Controlled Depth-Scaling on a 1-Lipschitz Family

Demonstrate sub-exponential scaling of ours vs the BaB-tree blowup
of αβ-CROWN and the abstractor saturation of NeuralSAT, on a
controlled synthetic family where every instance is **UNSAT by
construction**.

## Network family

7 spectrally-normalized ReLU MLPs:
* fixed width `W = 512`
* depths `D ∈ {2, 4, 8, 16, 24, 32, 40}`
* parameter count: ~5K (D=2) → ~10M (D=40)
* one network per depth (no init-seed variation)

## Per-network instances (10 each, 70 total)

* `x_0 ~ Uniform([-1, 1]^5)` — random "starting sample"
* input box: `[x_0 - 0.1, x_0 + 0.1]^5`
* threshold: `C = empirical_max(network output over 10K box samples) + 0.1`
* spec: `y > C` (UNSAT-by-construction since the 1-Lipschitz network
  bounds output growth and `C` is set above the empirical max +
  threshold margin)

`ground_truth = unsat` for every instance. A `verdict = UNSAT` is
correct; `UNKNOWN` is honest abstention; `SAT` would be a soundness
violation in the verifier.

## Methods (all 4 run by us)

| Method | Method tag | Configuration |
|---|---|---|
| ours (bounded AMLS) | `ours` | `mega + amls_bounded + max_levels=30` |
| Hashemi-clipping (m=8000) | `hashemi_clipping` | fixed across depths |
| αβ-CROWN | `alpha_beta_crown` | [`abcrown_exp4_deep_mlp.yaml`](abcrown_exp4_deep_mlp.yaml) — bs=1024, `pgd_order: skip`, input-split BaB. Plus `PYTORCH_CUDA_ALLOC_CONF=expandable_segments:True` for OOM mitigation |
| NeuralSAT | `neuralsat` | default + `--device cuda` |

## Per-instance timeout

600 s per (network, instance, method).

## Special-case sound-verifier behavior at depth

These are **expected**, not bugs — the experiment is *designed* to
show that current sound verifiers fail to scale. Document them
precisely so paper claims don't overreach.

### αβ-CROWN: BaB-tree blowup at depth ≥ 24

* d=2,4,8: UNSAT in 8.9–9.2 s
* d=16: UNSAT in 38.4 s
* d=24, 32, 40: TIMEOUT at 600 s

Internal logs at d=24 show **genuine exponential blowup**: worst-case
lower bound stalls at −6613.15 across iterations 18→21 (no monotone
improvement), open-domain count stable at 8K–9K (BaB generating new
branches as fast as it closes them), per-iteration bounding cost
constant at ~40 s. More time would not help.

### NeuralSAT: abstractor saturation at depth ≥ 16

* d=2, 4, 8: UNSAT in 16–19 s
* d=16, 24, 32, 40: UNKNOWN in ~17 s (constant wall regardless of depth)

NeuralSAT's CROWN abstractor produces vacuous initial bounds
(~−1e8) on the deeper networks, recognizes that no amount of BaB
can refine bounds that loose, and abandons before entering BaB.
This is **not** exponential blowup — it's a different failure mode
(abstractor too loose). NeuralSAT writes `early_stop` to its
result file, which our pipeline maps to UNKNOWN (semantically: "the
verifier could not decide").

### Required ONNX hygiene

αβ-CROWN's `onnx2pytorch` parser mishandles `Identity` ops on
duplicated parameter tensors that PyTorch's ONNX exporter emits at
depth ≥ 8 (e.g., 6 spurious `Identity(net.0.bias)` nodes at d=8
caused a `Linear.forward() takes 2 positional arguments but 3 were
given` error). Our [`networks.py:export_onnx`](networks.py) uses
`do_constant_folding=True` and post-processes with
`onnxsim.simplify` to strip these. Without that, αβ-CROWN errors
out before BaB starts and the scaling-limit signal is masked.
